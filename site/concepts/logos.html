Logos (Divine Word) PHILOSOPHICAL

Divine Reason, Creative Speech, λόγος

Rational principle of creation; divine utterance that structures reality; intermediary between transcendent One and material cosmos.

Logos (Divine Word) serves in Hermetic metaphysics as the creative intermediary between transcendent Nous and structured material cosmos. In the Poimandres (CH I), the Logos leaps forth from primal light onto undifferentiated Nature, causing elemental separation: fire rises upward, air follows, earth and water remain. It is both the rational order of creation and the active speech-act establishing that order.

Within the emanative hierarchy reconstructed from Iamblichus and the Corpus Hermeticum, Logos proceeds from Nous and is closely associated with demiurgic activity. Creation follows the sequence: primal Nous generates Logos, Logos structures Nature into four elements, ordered cosmos results. Logos occupies the pivot between intelligible reality and sensible creation.

The Hermetic Logos resonates with but remains distinct from Stoic, Platonic, and Christian uses. Unlike Stoic logos spermatikos, it emanates from a transcendent source. Unlike the Johannine Logos, it does not incarnate historically. The initiate perceives and participates in Logos through gnosis.

Significance

Logos is the mediating principle making Hermetic cosmology dynamic. It connects transcendent unity to material multiplicity and provides the rational structure apprehensible through gnosis, linking Hermetic thought to Stoic, Platonic, and Christian philosophical traditions.
