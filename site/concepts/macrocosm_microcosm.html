Macrocosm and Microcosm COSMOLOGICAL

as above so below, correspondence

The principle that the structure of the human being (microcosm) mirrors the structure of the universe (macrocosm).

The macrocosm-microcosm correspondence holds that human structure mirrors and participates in cosmic structure. The Emerald Tablet's most celebrated passage formulates this: that which is above is like that which is below, for the miracles of the One Thing. This is ontological participation, not mere analogy.

Because the Anthropos descended through celestial spheres incorporating their qualities, humans genuinely contain the cosmos. Unlike Gnostic systems viewing the world as flawed, Hermeticism embraces the visible cosmos as divine reflection. Operations at the microcosmic scale -- in the laboratory, through ritual, via astrological timing -- genuinely participate in macrocosmic processes.

The principle draws on traditions traceable to Nemesius of Emesa, transmitted through late antique Neoplatonism and embedded in the Sirr al-Khaliqa. It justifies the entire range of Hermetic operative practices and remains the Emerald Tablet's most enduring contribution to Western intellectual history.

Significance

The macrocosm-microcosm axiom is the Emerald Tablet's most famous teaching and the metaphysical foundation for all operative Hermeticism, establishing that human action participates in cosmic processes through structural correspondence.
