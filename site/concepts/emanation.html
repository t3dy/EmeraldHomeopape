Emanation (Exitus a Deo) THEOLOGICAL

Neoplatonic emanation, the One, Nous

The Neoplatonic doctrine that material multiplicity cascades from a singular, indivisible source.

Emanation (exitus a Deo) holds that all reality flows from a singular transcendent source through successive levels of being. The Emerald Tablet encapsulates this: as all things originated from One, so all were born from this unity by adaptation. Creation proceeds from the One through Nous, which generates the Demiurge, who fashions elements into ordered cosmos.

The Logos mediates between intelligible and sensible worlds, giving form to the formless. This parallels Neoplatonic metaphysics while retaining distinctly Hermetic features, particularly the positive valuation of materiality as the legitimate endpoint of divine self-expression rather than a degradation.

Alchemical practice reverses emanation: the substance ascends from earth to heaven (sublimation) then descends again (fixation), uniting superior and inferior forces. This circular movement transforms alchemy from metallurgy into participatory cosmology, enacting the Tablet's teaching that mastery requires ascending and descending through all levels of reality.

Significance

Emanation provides the metaphysical architecture making alchemy a spiritual discipline. By overlaying Aristotelian matter theory with Neoplatonic structures of descent and return, it frames the Emerald Tablet's operations as recapitulations of cosmic creation.
