Nous (Divine Mind) PHILOSOPHICAL

Divine Intelligence, νοῦς

The supreme intellect; first principle of being below the One; consciousness as cosmic principle.

Nous (Divine Mind) holds the highest position in the Hermetic hierarchy below the ineffable One. In the Poimandres (CH I), Nous reveals itself as the first god and source of life and light, generating Logos and the Demiurge. It is also the faculty within humans enabling direct apprehension of divine reality.

Bull's analysis identifies multiple levels of nous in the Hermetic system: the self-begotten Monad (Kmeph), the demiurgic nous (Amun-Ptah-Osiris), and nous present within human souls. The Definitions of Hermes distinguish divine from psychic nous, noting that not every soul possesses even the latter. Gnosis is available only to those who have received or awakened divine nous within themselves.

Nous holds the entire Hermetic system together: it is the source from which Logos proceeds, the faculty through which gnosis is achieved, the divine element the Anthropos carries into embodiment, and the goal toward which palingenesia aspires. Its prominence across virtually every treatise makes it the architectonic concept of Hermetic philosophy.

Significance

Nous is the architectonic concept of Hermetic metaphysics, connecting cosmology (creative source), anthropology (divine element in humans), and soteriology (faculty enabling gnosis). Its semantic range and frequency make it a primary marker for computational analysis of Hermetic texts.
