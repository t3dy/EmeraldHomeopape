Anthropos (Primal Human) PHILOSOPHICAL

Divine Humanity, Ἄνθρωπος

Original archetypal human containing the microcosm of all creation; human nature as essentially divine.

The Anthropos, or Primal Human, is a divine archetype who contains within itself the totality of creation. In the Poimandres (CH I), the Anthropos descends through the celestial spheres into material embodiment, driven by desire for Nature's beauty. This is not a fall into sin but creative participation that endows humans with an essentially divine constitution.

Within Hermetic thought, the Anthropos grounds the doctrine that humans possess nous alongside soul and body. As Bull demonstrates, the descent involves accretion of planetary qualities onto an originally noetic being, yielding a creature retaining both demiurgic authority and capacity for self-knowledge. The Definitions of Hermes further distinguish divine from psychic nous within humans.

The Anthropos connects to gnosis (recognizing divine origin), palingenesia (shedding material torments), and macrocosm-microcosm correspondence. Because humans contain the cosmos, the Hermetic magus can affect celestial realities through terrestrial operations.

Significance

The Anthropos establishes the Hermetic claim that human nature is fundamentally divine and capable of deification. It provides the anthropological foundation for operative Hermeticism: because humans mirror the cosmos, alchemical and theurgic work participates in macrocosmic transformation.
