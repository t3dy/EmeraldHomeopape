Four Elemental Qualities PHILOSOPHICAL

hot/cold/moist/dry, Aristotelian physics, hylomorphism

The four primary qualities (hot, cold, moist, dry) that combine to form the four elements and drive all material transformations.

The four elemental qualities -- hot, cold, moist, dry -- form the Aristotelian substrate of Hermetic natural philosophy. Elements are secondary composites of quality-pairs: fire is hot-dry, air hot-moist, water cold-moist, earth cold-dry. All material properties derive from interactions and proportions of these four qualities.

The Sirr al-Khaliqa, within which the Emerald Tablet is embedded, grounds all natural phenomena in this framework, deriving properties of minerals, plants, and animals deductively from qualitative proportions emerging from primordial undifferentiated matter. The Poimandres presents a parallel cosmogony where logos causes fire to rise from primordial water, followed by air, while earth and water remain.

This theory provides the physical substrate for the Tablet's alchemical instructions. Separation of subtle from gross, ascent and descent, and recombination of purified elements all become intelligible as operations on the balance of primary qualities within matter.

Significance

The four elemental qualities provide the material-theoretical framework within which the Emerald Tablet's instructions for separation, purification, and recombination become physically intelligible, bridging Hermetic cosmology and practical laboratory operation.
