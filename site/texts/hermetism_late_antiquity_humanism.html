Hermetism from Late Antiquity to Humanism (IPM 40) COMPILATION

ENGLISH · COMPILATION · 2003–2003 CE

Hermetism from Late Antiquity to Humanism (Instrumenta Patristica et Mediaevalia 40) is a scholarly volume of conference proceedings published by Brepols, edited by Paolo Lucentini, Ilaria Parri, and Vittoria Perrone Compagni. It collects the papers delivered at the international conference organized by the Hermes Latinus research group in Naples from November 20 to 24, 2001.

The volume is organized into four thematic sections: Philosophical Hermetism, Medieval and Renaissance Hermetism, Arabic and Hebrew Hermetism, and Operative Hermetism (covering alchemy, astrology, and magic). It was the first major conference to address the full breadth of textual and historiographical problems related to the diffusion of Hermeticism in medieval culture, bringing together scholars of diverse disciplinary backgrounds and linguistic expertise.

The publication is a landmark in the modern study of Hermeticism because it moved scholarly attention beyond the well-known Renaissance reception of the Corpus Hermeticum to examine the medieval Latin, Arabic, and Hebrew transmission networks that sustained Hermetic ideas throughout the Middle Ages. The Hermes Latinus project, which organized the conference, has been responsible for critical editions of numerous medieval Hermetic texts, including De sex rerum principiis and the Liber XXIV Philosophorum.

This volume is essential for researchers seeking to understand Hermeticism as a continuous intellectual tradition extending from late antiquity through the medieval period, rather than as a phenomenon that appeared suddenly with Ficino's Renaissance translations.
