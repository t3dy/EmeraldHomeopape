Zosimos of Panopolis AUTHOR

ca. 300 CE · AUTHOR

Also known as: Zosimos

Zosimos of Panopolis (fl. c. 300 CE) was a Greco-Egyptian alchemist active in Upper Egypt who stands at the critical juncture between Hermetic philosophy and practical laboratory work. He was among the most important figures in the formative period of the alchemical tradition.

Zosimos compiled the Cheirometica, a twenty-eight-book chemical encyclopedia systematizing alchemical knowledge. He drew on earlier authorities, particularly Demokritos (Pseudo-Democritus) and Maria the Jewess, combining practical recipes with theoretical discussions grounded in Hermetic cosmology.

His most celebrated writings are the allegorical Visions, dream narratives encoding alchemical operations within mystical and sacrificial imagery. These texts depict dissolution, purification, and transformation through vivid scenes of ritual dismemberment and renewal, representing the earliest extended allegorical treatment of alchemical transformation.

Zosimos is crucial for understanding how Hermetic religious ideas—the transmutation of the soul, macrocosm-microcosm correspondence, the authority of Egyptian wisdom—became embedded in the practical craft tradition that would eventually give rise to modern chemistry. His testimony provides important contemporary evidence for the Hermetic milieu of late antique Egypt.
