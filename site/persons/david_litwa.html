M. David Litwa SCHOLAR

contemporary · SCHOLAR

M. David Litwa is a contemporary scholar of ancient Mediterranean religions whose 2018 volume Hermetica II provides new English translations of the Hermetic fragments, excerpts, and testimonies that complement Brian Copenhaver's foundational 1992 translation of the Corpus Hermeticum and Asclepius. Trained in the study of early Christianity and Greco-Roman religion, Litwa brings particular sensitivity to the literary and narrative dimensions of the Hermetic texts.

Hermetica II assembles material that had previously been scattered or unavailable in English translation: the twenty-nine Stobaean excerpts (including the Kore Kosmou), the Oxford and Vienna papyrus fragments translated into English for the first time, and an expanded selection of fragments from authors including Zosimus of Panopolis, Augustine, and Albert the Great. The volume also gathers testimonies about Hermes Trismegistus from thirty-eight ancient and medieval authors, ranging from Cicero and Pseudo-Manetho to Al-Kindi, Michael Psellus, and Nicholas of Cusa.

Litwa's contribution emphasizes the fragmentary and constructed nature of Hermetic reception, showing how knowledge of Hermes Trismegistus was transmitted not only through the major treatises but through a diffuse web of excerpts, citations, and allusions across diverse intellectual traditions. His work, conceived as the necessary complement to Copenhaver's Hermetica, ensures that English-language scholarship now has access to the full range of ancient Hermetic textual evidence.
