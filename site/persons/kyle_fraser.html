Kyle Fraser SCHOLAR

contemporary · SCHOLAR

Kyle Fraser is a contemporary scholar whose work bridges the study of Iamblichean Neoplatonism and the Hermetic tradition. His research has brought fresh attention to the deep connections between these two currents of late antique religious thought.

His most significant publication is the article "Hermetism and Hieratic Tradition in the De mysteriis of Iamblichus," published in Greek, Roman, and Byzantine Studies (vol. 65, 2025). In this study, Fraser argues that Iamblichus's De mysteriis should be read in close connection with Hermetic texts, demonstrating that Iamblichus, writing under the pseudonym Abamon, positioned himself as a transmitter of ancient Egyptian priestly wisdom in a manner directly parallel to Hermetic pseudonymity.

Fraser identifies shared technical vocabulary between the De mysteriis and Hermetic writings, including the rare term exitalos found in both Corpus Hermeticum XVI and Iamblichus. He also develops a detailed analysis of the role of barbarous names (onomata barbara) in both traditions, showing how Iamblichus's defense of untranslatable ritual language reflects Hermetic concerns about the loss of sacral power in translation. His work has challenged conventional scholarly boundaries between Neoplatonic theurgy and Hermetic religion.
