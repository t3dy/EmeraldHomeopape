Michael Maier AUTHOR

early 17th c. CE · 1568–1622 · AUTHOR

Michael Maier (1568–1622) was a German physician, alchemist, and counselor to Emperor Rudolf II who produced some of the most visually and musically inventive works in the alchemical tradition. He was one of the rare seventeenth-century figures who drew on both the philosophical Hermeticism of the Corpus Hermeticum and the alchemical Hermeticism of the Emerald Tablet.

His masterwork, Atalanta Fugiens (1618), is a multimedia alchemical emblem book containing fifty emblems, each accompanied by an engraving, an epigram, and a musical fugue. The first emblem is dedicated to the Emerald Tablet's phrase about the wind carrying the philosophical child in its womb, establishing the Tablet as the foundation of his alchemical program. The work synthesizes classical mythology, Hermetic philosophy, and practical alchemical instruction in an unprecedented artistic form.

Maier, along with his contemporary Athanasius Kircher, represented an exceptional integration of the two main streams of Hermeticism—the philosophical tradition centered on the Corpus Hermeticum and the practical-alchemical tradition centered on the Tabula Smaragdina—that had largely developed independently since antiquity. His work remains a landmark in the history of Western esotericism and alchemical visual culture.
