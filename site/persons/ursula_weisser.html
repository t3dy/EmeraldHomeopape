Ursula Weisser SCHOLAR

20th c. · SCHOLAR

Ursula Weisser is a twentieth-century German scholar of Arabic science and medicine whose critical edition of the Sirr al-Khaliqa (Book of the Secret of Creation) represents a landmark in the study of the Emerald Tablet and its textual context. Her edition was published in 1979 by the Aleppo Institute for the History of Arabic Science.

Weisser's work originated as a doctoral dissertation completed in 1974 at the University of Frankfurt under the supervision of Fuat Sezgin, the renowned historian of Arabic-Islamic science. She analyzed over twenty Arabic manuscripts of the Sirr al-Khaliqa, establishing two main recensions (designated A and B) and providing the first comprehensive critical apparatus for the text.

The Sirr al-Khaliqa, attributed pseudepigraphically to Apollonius of Tyana (Balinas), is a cosmological encyclopedia structured around the six days of creation that contains the earliest known version of the Emerald Tablet as an appendix. Weisser's edition made this foundational text accessible for modern scholarly analysis and confirmed that it drew on heterogeneous late antique sources including Greek, Syriac, and Hermetic traditions. Her work remains the standard critical edition and an essential reference for all research on the Tablet's origins.
