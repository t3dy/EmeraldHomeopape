Julius Ruska SCHOLAR

early 20th c. · 1867–1949 · SCHOLAR

Julius Ruska (1867–1949) was a German philologist and historian of science whose foundational scholarship established the modern study of the Emerald Tablet as a textual and historical object. His work laid the groundwork for all subsequent investigation into the Tablet's origins and transmission.

Ruska's landmark publication, Tabula Smaragdina: Ein Beitrag zur Geschichte der hermetischen Literatur (1926), systematically traced the Arabic and Latin textual history of the Emerald Tablet. He identified and analyzed multiple manuscript traditions, established the relationships between different recensions, and proposed a date of composition between approximately 600 and 750 CE. He also discovered a twelfth-century Arabic recension attributed to Sagijus (Sergius) of Nablus.

Beyond the Emerald Tablet, Ruska made major contributions to the study of Arabic alchemy more broadly, including studies of al-Razi's Book of Secrets and other foundational texts. His philological methods brought rigorous textual criticism to a field that had previously been dominated by speculative and occultist approaches. Paul Kraus eulogized him as a pioneering figure in the scientific study of Arabic alchemical literature.
