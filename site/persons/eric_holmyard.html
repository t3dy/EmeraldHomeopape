Eric John Holmyard SCHOLAR

early 20th c. · 1891–1959 · SCHOLAR

Eric John Holmyard (1891-1959) was a British historian of science and chemistry whose pioneering work on Arabic alchemy in the early twentieth century transformed scholarly understanding of the Emerald Tablet's textual history. A schoolmaster and independent scholar, Holmyard combined linguistic competence in Arabic with a chemist's understanding of laboratory practice to produce groundbreaking studies of the Arabic alchemical corpus.

Holmyard's most significant contribution to the Hermetic tradition was his discovery of an early Arabic version of the Emerald Tablet embedded in the Kitab Ustuqus al-Uss al-Thani (Second Book of the Elements of Foundation), attributed to Jabir ibn Hayyan. This finding, which predated the previously known attestation in the Sirr al-Khaliqa, established the Jabirian corpus as a key early witness to the Tablet's circulation in Arabic and pushed back the earliest datable Arabic attestation of the text.

Holmyard's broader contributions include his work on Jabir ibn Hayyan and his general history Alchemy (1957), which provided an accessible survey of the alchemical tradition from antiquity through the early modern period. His textual discoveries remain foundational for the study of the Emerald Tablet's transmission history and are regularly cited in contemporary scholarship on the Arabic Hermetic tradition.
