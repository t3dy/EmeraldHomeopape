Peter J. Forshaw SCHOLAR

contemporary · SCHOLAR

Peter J. Forshaw is a contemporary scholar of early modern Hermeticism and alchemical culture, based at the University of Amsterdam. He is a leading authority on the visual, material, and intellectual dimensions of Hermetic-alchemical practice in the sixteenth and seventeenth centuries.

Forshaw's major work is The Mage's Images: Heinrich Khunrath in His Oratory and Laboratory (Brill, 2 volumes), a comprehensive study of the alchemist and theosopher Heinrich Khunrath (1560–1605). This work examines Khunrath's Amphitheatrum Sapientiae Aeternae and related texts and images in their full intellectual context, including their connections to the Hermetic tradition, Lullian art, Paracelsian medicine, and Kabbalistic thought.

Forshaw's research has shed light on the relationship between figures like Khunrath, John Dee, and Michael Maier, demonstrating the networks of influence and patronage that connected Hermetic practitioners across early modern Europe. His work on Hermetic visual culture—emblems, diagrams, and symbolic engravings—has contributed to understanding how alchemical and Hermetic ideas were communicated through non-textual media.
