Brian P. Copenhaver SCHOLAR

contemporary · SCHOLAR

Brian P. Copenhaver is an American scholar of Renaissance philosophy and the history of Western esotericism whose 1992 volume Hermetica -- providing the standard English translation of the Corpus Hermeticum and the Latin Asclepius with extensive scholarly introduction and notes -- established the modern benchmark for anglophone access to the philosophical Hermetic texts. Based at UCLA, Copenhaver brought rigorous philosophical training to a body of texts that had often been treated impressionistically.

Copenhaver's translation emphasizes the philosophical substance of the Hermetic dialogues, situating them within the context of Middle Platonic, Stoic, and Gnostic thought in the Roman imperial period. His commentary draws attention to the internal diversity of the Corpus Hermeticum, resisting the temptation to read it as a unified theological system. The Hermetica volume also includes a magisterial introduction tracing the reception history from antiquity through the Renaissance.

Copenhaver's legacy is defined by the enduring centrality of his translation as the primary scholarly reference. The subsequent publication of Hermetica II by M. David Litwa, which gathers the Stobaean excerpts, papyrus fragments, and testimonies not included in Copenhaver's volume, was explicitly conceived as a complement to and completion of Copenhaver's foundational work. Together, these two volumes constitute the essential English-language scholarly edition of the Hermetic textual tradition.
