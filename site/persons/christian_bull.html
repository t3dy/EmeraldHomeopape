Christian H. Bull SCHOLAR

contemporary · SCHOLAR

Christian H. Bull is a Norwegian scholar of ancient religion whose 2018 monograph The Tradition of Hermes Trismegistus: The Egyptian Priestly Figure as a Teacher of Hellenized Wisdom represents one of the most significant recent contributions to the study of Hermeticism. Based at the University of Oslo and active in the study of Nag Hammadi and related corpora, Bull brings expertise in both Egyptology and Greco-Roman religion to the Hermetic texts.

Bull's central argument challenges the longstanding scholarly tendency to treat the Hermetic texts as purely Greek philosophical compositions that merely borrowed an Egyptian pseudepigraphic frame. Instead, he makes the case for genuine Egyptian priestly authorship, arguing that the Hermetic dialogues reflect actual Egyptian temple practices, ritual traditions, and theological concepts translated into Greek philosophical idiom. He places particular emphasis on the ritual dimensions of the texts, connecting Hermetic initiation practices to documented Egyptian temple ceremonies.

Bull's work stands as a significant corrective to the dominant tradition in Hermetic scholarship running from Festugiere through Copenhaver, which tended to minimize the Egyptian element in favor of Greek philosophical analysis. His emphasis on Egyptian authenticity and ritual practice aligns with and extends the earlier work of Garth Fowden while incorporating insights from the Nag Hammadi Hermetic texts and recent advances in Egyptology.
