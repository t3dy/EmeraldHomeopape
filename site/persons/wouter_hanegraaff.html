Wouter J. Hanegraaff SCHOLAR

contemporary · SCHOLAR

Wouter J. Hanegraaff is a contemporary Dutch scholar and one of the leading figures in the academic study of Western esotericism. He holds a chair at the University of Amsterdam, where he helped establish the field as a recognized academic discipline.

Hanegraaff has contributed to Hermetic studies through both editorial work and original scholarship. With Ruud Bouthoorn, he prepared a critical edition of the Hermetic writings of Lodovico Lazzarelli, making this important body of Renaissance Hermetic literature available for scholarly analysis. His broader work on the history and methodology of esotericism studies has provided essential theoretical frameworks for contextualizing Hermeticism within Western intellectual history.

His 2022 monograph, Hermetic Spirituality and the Historical Imagination, represents a major reinterpretation of the Hermetic tradition, emphasizing its visionary and transformative dimensions. Hanegraaff argues for understanding Hermeticism not merely as a set of philosophical doctrines but as a lived spiritual practice centered on the experience of gnosis and inner transformation. This approach has enriched the scholarly understanding of what Hermetic texts were for—not merely read but enacted—and has influenced the broader field of religious studies.
