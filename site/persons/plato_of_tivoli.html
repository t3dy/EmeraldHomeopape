Plato of Tivoli TRANSLATOR

12th c. CE · TRANSLATOR

Also known as: Plato Tiburtinus

Plato of Tivoli (Plato Tiburtinus, fl. c. 1134–1145) was an Italian mathematician, astronomer, and translator active in Barcelona during the twelfth-century translation movement. He worked alongside the Jewish scholar Abraham bar Hiyya to render Arabic scientific and mathematical texts into Latin.

Plato of Tivoli is widely credited with producing the Vulgate Latin version of the Emerald Tablet, the translation that became the standard text throughout the medieval and early modern periods. This attribution, while conjectural, is accepted by many scholars based on the dating and provenance of the earliest Latin manuscripts. His translation may have drawn on different Arabic sources than those used by other translators such as Hugh of Santalla.

The Vulgate Emerald Tablet circulated independently and was incorporated into various alchemical anthologies and compendia, becoming the most frequently cited version of the Tablet in Western alchemical literature. Plato's translation work thus played a crucial role in establishing the Emerald Tablet as a foundational text of Latin alchemy.
