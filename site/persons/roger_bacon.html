Roger Bacon COMMENTATOR

13th c. CE · 1214–1292 · COMMENTATOR

Also known as: Doctor Mirabilis

Roger Bacon (c. 1214–1292) was an English Franciscan friar, philosopher, and early advocate of experimental science who made significant contributions to the medieval reception of the Emerald Tablet. He was among the most learned scholars of the thirteenth century, with expertise spanning optics, mathematics, languages, and natural philosophy.

Bacon composed an extensive commentary on Philip of Tripoli's Latin translation of the pseudo-Aristotelian Secretum Secretorum, which contained a version of the Emerald Tablet. His commentary helped establish the Tablet as a legitimate object of scholarly attention within the medieval university system, lending it the weight of Franciscan intellectual authority.

Bacon's engagement with the Vulgate Emerald Tablet reflects the broader pattern by which Hermetic and alchemical texts gained credibility in medieval Europe through association with Aristotelian philosophy and scholastic commentary. His work contributed to the integration of Hermetic natural philosophy into the mainstream of medieval Latin learning.
