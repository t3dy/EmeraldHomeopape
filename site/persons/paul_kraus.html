Paul Kraus SCHOLAR

mid-20th c. · 1904–1944 · SCHOLAR

Paul Kraus (1904–1944) was a Czech-born historian of science and Arabist whose brief but extraordinarily productive career produced foundational scholarship on Arabic alchemy and its relationship to the Hermetic tradition. His work, cut short by his early death, remains indispensable for the study of the Jabirian corpus and the Sirr al-Khaliqa.

Kraus's most important contribution was his demonstration that the massive corpus attributed to Jabir ibn Hayyan was likely not the work of a single author but rather a collective pseudepigraphical effort, possibly produced by writers with Ismaili affiliations over an extended period. His monumental study Jabir ibn Hayyan: Contribution a l'histoire des idees scientifiques dans l'Islam analyzed the philosophical and doctrinal content of the corpus with unprecedented rigor.

Kraus also made crucial contributions to understanding the Sirr al-Khaliqa (Book of the Secret of Creation), the text containing the earliest known version of the Emerald Tablet. He established that this text shared sources with the Syriac Book of Treasures of Job of Edessa and incorporated material from Nemesius of Emesa's De Natura Hominis, providing key evidence for dating and locating the Tablet's composition. His analysis of the Sirr al-Khaliqa as providing the theoretical framework for the alchemical operations encoded in the Emerald Tablet remains influential.
