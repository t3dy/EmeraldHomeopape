Job of Odessa AUTHOR

9th c. CE · AUTHOR

Also known as: Ayyub al-Ruhawi

Job of Odessa (also known as Job of Edessa; Ayyub al-Ruhawi, d. 835 CE) was a ninth-century Syriac Christian scholar and natural philosopher. He is the author of the Book of Treasures (Kitab al-Dhakhair), an encyclopedic work on natural philosophy composed in Syriac.

Job's significance for the Hermetic tradition lies in the textual relationship between his Book of Treasures and the Arabic Sirr al-Khaliqa (Book of the Secret of Creation), the text attributed to Pseudo-Apollonius of Tyana (Balinas) that contains the earliest known version of the Emerald Tablet. Scholars, notably Paul Kraus, have established that the Sirr al-Khaliqa shares a problemataphysica source with Job's work, suggesting both drew on common late antique Syriac scientific traditions.

While the Book of Treasures itself does not contain the Emerald Tablet, the shared source material between Job's work and the Sirr al-Khaliqa points to a Syriac intermediary stage in the transmission of Hermetic and alchemical knowledge from Greek late antiquity into the Arabic world. Job's work thus serves as important circumstantial evidence for reconstructing the textual history of the Emerald Tablet and its embedding within a broader cosmological encyclopedia.
