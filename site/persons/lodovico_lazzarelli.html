Lodovico Lazzarelli AUTHOR

late 15th c. CE · 1447–1500 · AUTHOR

Also known as: Lazarelli

Lodovico Lazzarelli (1447–1500) was an Italian humanist poet and Hermetic philosopher who represents a crucial bridge between medieval and Renaissance Hermeticism. Active in the late fifteenth century, he was one of the few Renaissance figures to compose original Hermetic writings rather than merely commenting on or translating ancient texts.

Lazzarelli's Hermetic works, studied in the critical edition prepared by Wouter Hanegraaff and Ruud Bouthoorn for the ACMRS series, reveal an active engagement with the Corpus Hermeticum that went beyond scholarly commentary. He sought to renew and extend the Hermetic tradition as a living spiritual practice, composing dialogues and treatises that positioned him as a latter-day participant in the Hermetic chain of transmission.

His significance lies in demonstrating that Renaissance Hermeticism was not merely an antiquarian recovery of ancient texts but could generate new creative and spiritual output. Lazzarelli's work has received increasing scholarly attention as part of the broader reassessment of Western esotericism as a continuous, creative intellectual tradition rather than a mere repository of ancient learning.
