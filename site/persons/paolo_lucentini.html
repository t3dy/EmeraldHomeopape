Paolo Lucentini SCHOLAR

contemporary · SCHOLAR

Paolo Lucentini is a contemporary Italian scholar who has served as the leader of the Hermes Latinus research group, an international collaborative project dedicated to editing, cataloguing, and studying the medieval Latin Hermetic corpus. His work has been central to establishing the study of medieval Hermeticism as a rigorous scholarly field.

Lucentini co-edited the critical edition of De sex rerum principiis (On the Six Principles of Things) with Mark Damien Delp, an important medieval Latin Hermetic text. He also served as principal editor of the conference volume Hermetism from Late Antiquity to Humanism (Instrumenta Patristica et Mediaevalia 40, Brepols, 2003), which gathered contributions from an international conference held in Naples in 2001—the first to address the full range of textual and historiographical problems related to medieval Hermeticism.

Lucentini's editorial and organizational work has been instrumental in revealing the breadth and complexity of medieval Latin Hermetic transmission, demonstrating that Hermetic texts circulated far more widely in the medieval West than previously recognized. His Hermes Latinus project has produced catalogues and editions that provide essential tools for all subsequent research in the field.
