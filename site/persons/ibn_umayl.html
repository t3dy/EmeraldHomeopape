Ibn Umayl AUTHOR

10th c. CE · AUTHOR

Also known as: Senior Zadith

Ibn Umayl (d. c. 960 CE) was a tenth-century Arabic alchemist who occupies an important place in the transmission of Hermetic alchemical literature. He was one of the major alchemical authorities of the early Islamic period, alongside al-Razi and Jabir ibn Hayyan.

His principal work, the Book of the Silvery Water and the Starry Earth (Kitab al-Ma al-Waraqi wa-l-Ard al-Najmiyya), preserved a distinctive version of the Emerald Tablet discovery narrative. In his account, the Tablet was found within an Egyptian pyramid, offering an alternative to the cave-discovery narrative found in the Sirr al-Khaliqa attributed to Pseudo-Apollonius (Balinas).

Ibn Umayl's writings are significant for preserving early alchemical traditions that might otherwise have been lost. His work transmits older Hermetic material while situating it within the broader context of Islamic alchemical practice and theory. Scholars have recognized him as an important witness to the diversity of Emerald Tablet traditions circulating in the medieval Arabic world.
